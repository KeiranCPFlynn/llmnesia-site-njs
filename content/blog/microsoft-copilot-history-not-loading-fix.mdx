Microsoft Copilot's conversation history can fail to display — showing a blank pane, missing recent conversations, or a sidebar that spins without loading. In most cases the conversations are not lost; there's a loading or authentication issue that can be resolved.

This guide covers every known cause and the specific fix for each.

## Step 1: Check if Microsoft services are down

Before troubleshooting locally, confirm the problem isn't on Microsoft's side.

1. Go to **status.microsoft.com** or the **Microsoft 365 Service Health** dashboard
2. Check if Copilot or related services show any incidents or degraded performance
3. On the Microsoft Edge browser, the Copilot icon in the toolbar may show a status indicator if there's a known issue

If Microsoft's services are degraded, wait for the incident to resolve. History will return once service is restored.

## Step 2: Hard refresh the page

The simplest fix for intermittent loading problems:

- **Windows/Linux:** Ctrl + Shift + R
- **Mac:** Cmd + Shift + R

This forces the browser to reload the page without using cached assets. A large percentage of loading issues resolve with this single step.

## Step 3: Test in incognito mode

Open a new incognito/private window, go to **copilot.microsoft.com**, and sign in:

- **Chrome:** Ctrl+Shift+N / Cmd+Shift+N
- **Firefox:** Ctrl+Shift+P / Cmd+Shift+P
- **Edge:** Ctrl+Shift+N / Cmd+Shift+N (InPrivate mode)

Incognito disables extensions by default. If history loads in incognito but not your regular window, a browser extension is causing the problem.

**Common culprits for Copilot specifically:**
- Ad blockers that include Microsoft or Bing domains in their block lists
- Tracking blockers that block Microsoft analytics scripts
- Privacy extensions that restrict cookie storage
- Other AI-related browser extensions

**To identify the extension causing the issue:**
1. Re-enable extensions one at a time in a regular window
2. Reload Copilot after enabling each
3. When history stops loading, the last extension you enabled is the cause
4. Add copilot.microsoft.com and microsoft.com to that extension's allowlist

## Step 4: Clear site data for Copilot

Corrupted session data or an expired authentication token can prevent history from loading.

**In Chrome:**
1. Navigate to copilot.microsoft.com
2. Click the padlock or tune icon in the address bar
3. Select "Site settings" or "Cookies and site data"
4. Click "Delete data" or "Clear data"
5. Reload the page and sign back in with your Microsoft account

**In Edge:**
1. Navigate to copilot.microsoft.com
2. Click the padlock icon in the address bar
3. Select "Cookies and site permissions" → "Manage and delete cookies and site data"
4. Clear data for microsoft.com

After clearing site data and signing back in, a fresh authentication session is established and history loading typically resumes.

## Step 5: Verify Microsoft account sign-in

Copilot history is tied to your Microsoft account. A common cause of missing history is an account session issue or being signed into the wrong account.

**Check your current account:**
1. Go to copilot.microsoft.com
2. Click your account icon in the top right
3. Verify the email address shown is the one you expected

**Sign out and sign back in:**
1. Click your account icon → Sign out
2. Close the browser tab
3. Reopen copilot.microsoft.com
4. Sign in fresh

A fresh sign-in resolves token expiry issues that can cause the history pane to appear empty.

## Step 6: Personal vs work account differences

Microsoft Copilot behaves differently on personal Microsoft accounts versus Microsoft 365 work or school accounts.

**Personal accounts (copilot.microsoft.com):** History is tied to your personal Microsoft account. Standard troubleshooting steps apply.

**Work/school accounts (Microsoft 365 Copilot):** Your organisation's IT policies govern history access, retention, and display. If Copilot is deployed through a work account:
- History may be managed or limited by IT policy
- Some history features may be disabled by your organisation's admin settings
- Contact your IT helpdesk if history is consistently not loading — it may be a configuration issue rather than a browser problem

**Switching between accounts:** If you use both a personal and work Microsoft account in the same browser, you may be inadvertently viewing Copilot on one account while your history is on the other. Check which account is active before concluding that history is missing.

## Step 7: Try Microsoft Edge

Copilot is developed by Microsoft and is most deeply integrated into the Edge browser. If you're using Chrome or another browser and experiencing persistent loading issues, testing in Edge can help determine if the problem is browser-specific.

If history loads in Edge but not Chrome:
- The issue is Chrome-specific
- Try updating Chrome to the latest version
- Clear Chrome's stored data for Microsoft domains
- Check Chrome's extension list for any extensions that might interfere with Microsoft's services

## Copilot history retention limits

Unlike some AI platforms that maintain indefinitely growing history, Microsoft Copilot has more limited conversation history retention. This is separate from a loading error.

If you're looking for conversations from months ago and they're simply not present in the history pane, Copilot may not retain conversations that old — particularly on the free tier.

For important Copilot conversations, the practical prevention is:
- Export conversations you want to keep (use the share or copy functionality within the conversation)
- Install LLMnesia, which indexes Copilot conversations locally as you have them — the local index persists regardless of Copilot's server-side retention

## Still not resolved?

If none of the above steps fix the issue:

1. Try accessing Copilot through the Microsoft 365 portal (office.com) if you have a work account
2. Clear all Microsoft-related cookies in your browser using the browser's full history clearing tools
3. Contact Microsoft Support — for persistent account-specific issues, Microsoft's support team can check account status

Most history loading issues are resolved by the incognito test and site data clear. If those don't work, the issue is typically account-specific or related to an active service incident.
