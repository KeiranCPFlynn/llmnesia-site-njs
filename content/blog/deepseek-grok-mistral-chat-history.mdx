DeepSeek, Grok, and Mistral have moved from experiment to daily workflow for a significant portion of AI users. Each offers something the established players don't: DeepSeek with its cost-efficiency and reasoning model, Grok with its real-time X data access, Mistral with its European data residency and open-weight models.

But all three launched their consumer interfaces quickly, and history management reflects that. Understanding what each platform offers — and what it doesn't — is increasingly important as these tools become part of a regular multi-platform workflow.

## DeepSeek: conversation history and privacy considerations

DeepSeek Chat at `chat.deepseek.com` saves conversations tied to your account. The sidebar provides a chronological history list. Navigation is possible but search is limited to what's visible in titles or auto-summaries — there is no full-text conversation search.

DeepSeek's R1 reasoning model and its pricing advantage (significantly cheaper than GPT-4 for API access) have made it popular for technical tasks, code generation, and research workflows. Users running heavy sessions accumulate conversation history quickly.

**Privacy note:** DeepSeek is operated by Hangzhou DeepSeek Artificial Intelligence, a Chinese company subject to Chinese data laws. Several organisations and national governments have restricted or banned DeepSeek use for work-related or sensitive content. If you use DeepSeek for anything involving client work, company-sensitive information, or personal data, verify your organisation's policy and review DeepSeek's privacy policy carefully.

For users who continue to use DeepSeek for appropriate tasks, the history retrieval limitations are the same as other platforms: title-only search, single-platform silo.

## Grok: real-time context, limited history management

Grok is accessible via `x.com/grok` and `grok.com`, tied to your X (formerly Twitter) account. Its primary differentiator is real-time access to X data — Grok can answer questions about recent posts, trends, and public conversations in a way that other AI tools can't.

History is accessible from the Grok sidebar. As with the other platforms in this article, native search is limited to title navigation. There is no built-in export or data portability feature comparable to ChatGPT's data export.

Grok users who use it primarily for research, trend tracking, or X-adjacent tasks often ask follow-up questions that build on previous sessions. Without search, those sessions become reference material you can't easily access.

## Mistral: European deployment, limited history interface

Mistral Chat at `chat.mistral.ai` provides access to Mistral's language models. Mistral is a French AI company, which matters for users and organisations that prefer or require EU-based data processing. Their models include open-weight variants that can be self-hosted.

The consumer chat interface has a standard sidebar history and basic navigation. Export options are more limited than ChatGPT or Claude at this time. For Mistral's target audience — technically sophisticated users and European enterprise — the absence of robust history management is a notable gap.

## The shared limitation

DeepSeek, Grok, and Mistral all share the same fundamental history problem that affects every AI platform: title-based navigation, single-platform silos, no full-text content search.

For users who add these tools to an existing ChatGPT and Claude workflow, this compounds the multi-platform retrieval problem. You now have five separate history interfaces. Remembering which tool you used for a specific task — and then searching within that tool — becomes a meaningful workflow friction.

## Searching across all platforms from one place

LLMnesia supports DeepSeek, Grok, and Mistral alongside ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and others. The extension indexes conversations from all supported platforms automatically as you browse them.

When you search in LLMnesia, results come from all indexed platforms simultaneously — you don't need to know which tool you used to find an answer. A single query surfaces results from every platform you've used, with jump-back links to the original conversations.

This is the only way to unify the fragmented history across a five-platform workflow without maintaining separate search habits for each tool.
