Poe is Quora's multi-model AI platform — it provides access to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Llama, and dozens of custom community-built bots from a single interface. For users who've accumulated significant conversation history across multiple bots, retrieving a specific past answer presents a unique challenge: the per-bot history organisation means you need to know which bot you used before you can start looking.

## How Poe organises conversation history

Unlike ChatGPT or Claude where all conversations appear in one unified sidebar, Poe organises history per-bot:

- Each bot maintains its own conversation history
- To see your conversations with ChatGPT on Poe, navigate to the ChatGPT bot and access its history
- To see conversations with Claude, navigate to the Claude bot, and so on
- There is no single "all conversations" view that shows everything across all bots

This architecture reflects Poe's identity as a platform that hosts multiple models. It's logical from an organisation standpoint but makes cross-bot retrieval difficult.

**Practical implication:** If you don't remember whether you had a conversation on Poe with Claude or with GPT-4, you need to check both bot histories separately. If you've used many different bots over time, finding one specific conversation could mean checking many individual bot histories.

## Method 1: Navigate to the specific bot's history

If you remember which bot you used:

1. Sign in to poe.com
2. Find the bot in your sidebar or through the bot directory
3. Open a chat with that bot
4. Browse the conversation history for that bot (usually accessible from within the chat interface)

For users with a small number of bots they regularly use, this is the most direct approach.

## Method 2: Check recent conversations from the sidebar

Poe shows recently accessed conversations (across all bots) in the main sidebar:

1. Sign in to poe.com
2. Look at the left sidebar which shows recent conversations
3. Scroll to find the conversation by title or recency

This is the closest thing to a unified history view — it shows recent activity across all bots, not just one. The limitation is that it's sorted by recency, so older conversations may require scrolling past many newer ones.

## Method 3: Browser history

For conversations accessed recently:

1. Open browser history (Ctrl+H)
2. Search for "poe.com"
3. Look for conversation URLs — Poe URLs often include bot names, making them more scannable than platforms with opaque IDs

Browser history is often the fastest path for conversations within the last few weeks, especially if you can associate the conversation with a specific date or project.

## Method 4: Browser Ctrl+F inside an open conversation

For finding a specific phrase within a conversation you've located:

1. Open the conversation
2. Use Ctrl+F to search within the visible content

For long conversations, this is the fastest way to jump to a specific section.

## Method 5: Search directly on the underlying platforms

The structural limitation of Poe's history is that there's no way to search across all bots and all conversations simultaneously. If you've used 10 different bots over the past year, finding a specific answer requires knowing (or guessing) which bot it came from.

One alternative: access the underlying models directly. If you use Claude on Poe, try searching your Claude history at claude.ai for the same topic — if you also use Claude directly, the answer may already be there. The same applies to ChatGPT and Gemini.

For users who access ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or Perplexity both through Poe and directly, LLMnesia indexes those direct-platform conversations and lets you search across all of them from one place — separate from Poe's per-bot archive.

## Poe-specific considerations

**Custom bots:** Poe allows creating and using custom bots (user-created bots with specific prompting). Conversations with custom bots are saved per-bot like any other. If the custom bot you used was later deleted (by its creator or yourself), you may not be able to access that bot's interface to view history — though the conversation itself may still be accessible via URL.

**Subscription limits:** Poe free tier limits usage of premium models (GPT-4, Claude 3 Opus, etc.). If you've used multiple models across different subscription periods, your history may span model versions that you no longer have access to use, but can still read.

**Cross-platform confusion:** Users who access Claude or ChatGPT both directly and via Poe can find their history split across platforms. Searching on Claude.ai won't find conversations you had with Claude on Poe. LLMnesia unifies both into one searchable index.

## Poe vs direct platform access: which history is better?

| Feature | Poe history | Direct platform history |
|---------|-------------|------------------------|
| Unified view across models | Partial (recent) | No (by definition) |
| Per-model organisation | Yes (per-bot) | Yes (single model) |
| Full-text search | No | No (native) |
| Export | No | Depends on platform |
| Access to all model versions | Yes | Limited to current version |

For users who primarily use Poe as their AI interface, the lack of cross-bot search is the most significant history limitation. Browser extension indexing is currently the only solution for this.
