## What ChatGPT's native history does

ChatGPT includes a sidebar with your conversation history, a basic title search, and the ability to rename and archive chats. For most users who just started using AI or work exclusively inside ChatGPT, this is functional.

Where it breaks down:

- **Volume**: Once you have hundreds of conversations, scrolling and searching by title becomes slow and unreliable.
- **Vague recall**: If you can't remember the title you gave a conversation (or didn't rename it), search fails.
- **Single platform**: It only covers ChatGPT. If you got an answer in Claude last week, ChatGPT's history can't find it.
- **No in-conversation search**: You can find a chat by title, but you can't search for a specific phrase *within* a conversation.

## What LLMnesia adds

LLMnesia is a Chrome extension that runs in the background on ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and other platforms. As you work, it indexes your conversations automatically. You search from a single interface that covers all of them.

- **Full-text search** across all indexed sessions — search a code snippet, a phrase from an answer, or a topic name.
- **Cross-platform** — one search covers ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and more simultaneously.
- **Jump-back links** — results include a direct link back to the original conversation on the original platform.
- **Local-first** — your index is stored on your device; your conversations are not sent to a third-party server.

## Where native ChatGPT history is still fine

If you use ChatGPT exclusively and you tend to work on a small number of focused projects with clearly-named conversations, native history is probably adequate. The friction only appears at scale and across tools.

## The multi-platform reality

Most people who start with one AI tool end up using two or three. ChatGPT for general tasks, Claude for long documents, Gemini for Google Workspace integrations. Once that happens, single-tool history search becomes a liability: you have to remember which tool you used before you can even start searching.

LLMnesia removes that prerequisite. You search once, and results come from wherever the answer actually was.

## Choosing between them

**Stick with native ChatGPT history if:**
- You use ChatGPT exclusively and rarely switch to other platforms.
- Your conversation volume is manageable and conversations are well-named.
- You need no cross-platform capability now or in the near future.

**Switch to LLMnesia if:**
- You regularly use more than one AI tool.
- You spend time re-asking questions you already solved.
- You want to search the content of conversations, not just their titles.
