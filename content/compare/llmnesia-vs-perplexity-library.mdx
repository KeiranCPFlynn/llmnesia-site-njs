## What Perplexity Library does

Perplexity is an AI-powered search engine that provides sourced, cited answers rather than just raw web links. Its Library feature saves your Perplexity threads — your searches and the AI's responses — so you can reference them later without re-searching.

For users who use Perplexity as their primary research tool, Library is a useful in-product memory. You can browse saved searches, revisit cited sources, and pick up where you left off on a research thread.

## The single-platform ceiling

Perplexity Library's value is entirely within the Perplexity ecosystem. This is fine for users whose research workflow lives exclusively in Perplexity. But most knowledge workers do not stay in one tool:

- Research in Perplexity
- Reasoning and synthesis in Claude
- Code and implementation in ChatGPT
- Writing in Claude or ChatGPT

The moment you leave Perplexity, the Library cannot help you find what you did next. And the moment a collaborator is involved, or a decision was made two tools ago, the Library is silent.

## What LLMnesia does differently

LLMnesia is a Chrome extension that indexes conversations across all supported AI platforms — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and others — into a single searchable index. You search once and get results from wherever the answer actually was.

Key differences:

- **Cross-platform** — one search covers all your AI tools, not just Perplexity.
- **Automatic indexing** — conversations are captured as you have them; no manual save step.
- **Jump-back links** — search results link to the original conversation on the original platform.
- **Local-first** — your index is stored on your device, not in a cloud account.

## The research-to-writing workflow

Here is a typical workflow where Perplexity Library falls short and LLMnesia helps:

1. You research a topic in Perplexity — findings are in Perplexity Library.
2. You synthesise in Claude — that session is not in Perplexity Library.
3. You draft in ChatGPT — that session is also not in Perplexity Library.
4. Three weeks later you need to find the original source citation from step 1 and the synthesis from step 2.

With Perplexity Library: you can find step 1, but step 2 requires scrolling Claude history manually.

With LLMnesia: you search "topic research [keyword]" and get results from both Perplexity and Claude in one view.

## Choosing between them

**Perplexity Library is the right choice if:**
- Your research workflow is exclusively within Perplexity.
- You primarily need to revisit Perplexity-specific cited sources.
- You do not regularly move between Perplexity and other AI tools.

**LLMnesia is the better fit if:**
- You use Perplexity alongside ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.
- You need to find information from any AI session, not just Perplexity threads.
- You want one search interface for your entire AI conversation history.
