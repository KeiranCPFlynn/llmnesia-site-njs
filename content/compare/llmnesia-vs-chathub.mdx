## What ChatHub actually is

ChatHub (200,000+ users, 4.7★) is a powerful multi-model interface. You open it and can query ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Llama, and 20+ other models simultaneously in a side-by-side view. It supports three access methods: your existing logged-in accounts, your own API keys, or a ChatHub subscription.

It also offers a local chat history feature with full-text search — but this only covers conversations you have *through ChatHub itself*.

## The critical distinction

This is the key thing to understand before comparing these two tools:

**ChatHub history** = conversations you ran through the ChatHub interface, stored locally within the extension.

**LLMnesia history** = conversations you had natively on chatgpt.com, claude.ai, gemini.google.com, and other platforms — indexed automatically in the background as you work.

If you use ChatGPT natively on chatgpt.com — which most users do — those sessions are invisible to ChatHub's history search. ChatHub only knows about the prompts you ran through its own interface.

## What ChatHub does well

- **Side-by-side model comparison** across 20+ AI services simultaneously.
- **Prompt library** — manage and discover frequently used prompts.
- **Local chat history with full-text search** for conversations run through ChatHub.
- **Smart web access** — enrich responses with live web results.
- **Custom chatbots** — integrate any OpenAI-compatible API.
- **Flexible access** — use your own accounts, API keys, or ChatHub's subscription service.

## What LLMnesia does differently

- **Indexes native sessions** — captures conversations from chatgpt.com, claude.ai, gemini.google.com and others as you use them normally.
- **No interface switch required** — you work in the platform you prefer; LLMnesia runs in the background.
- **Jump-back links** — results return you to the original conversation on the original platform.
- **Local-first** — your index stays on your device.

## Choosing between them

**Use ChatHub when:**
- You want to query multiple AI models simultaneously in one view.
- You are benchmarking model quality or comparing responses.
- You are willing to run prompts through ChatHub's interface to benefit from its history.

**Use LLMnesia when:**
- You work natively in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and want your full history indexed.
- You want retrieval to cover all your sessions, not just the ones run through a separate interface.
- Search is the primary need, not real-time model comparison.

**Use both when:**
- You do active multi-model comparison work *and* need searchable retrieval across all your native AI sessions.
